Swarm Intelligence


According to Wikipedia:

"Swarm intelligence (SI) is the collective behavior of decentralized, self-organized systems, natural or artificial."

In nature, it is a description for how large masses of entities behave together.

In software, it is the emulation of that same behaviour within nature.

For both, it is the resulting behaviour that results from a mass of entities following a set of rules.

Getting started with Swarm Intelligence


The basics of Swarm Intelligence lie in several key factors:

  • Cohesion
  • Separation
  • Alignment

Cohesion

Cohesion is what draws the entities of a swarm to one another.

It is the driving force that keeps the swarm's overall structure together!

Cohesion

Get the positions of all other entities

Add those positions together...

...and keep the total

Divide the total by the number of other entities for the center

Subtract the center from the current entity's position

The result is the cohesion force!

Cohesion - In Code!


Separation

Separation is what keeps entities from bumping into one another.

It's basically just what allows each member of the swarm their personal space!

Separation

For a given neighbor entity, take it's position and this entity's position...

...and subtract the other entity's position from this entity's position

Normalize the result and store it

Repeat this process for all other entities

Add the results all together

The result is the separation force!

Alignment

Alignment is what keeps the entities headed in the same direction

Alignment

For each entity, add it's velocity together...

...the sum of which gives the alignment force!
